Watch Out! Red Flags in Crypto and DePIN Projects to Avoid

By Zach Miller (Prime Moover) · Nov 5, 2024

The cryptocurrency and DePIN space is filled with opportunities, but also known for its risks. Here are the key red flags:

1. Promises of Guaranteed High Returns

No legitimate project can promise consistent, high returns. "Risk-free" profits are often Ponzi schemes.

2. Lack of a Clear Problem-Solution Fit

Projects without a concrete purpose rely on hype rather than substance.

3. Inexperienced or Anonymous Team

Anonymity makes it easier for bad actors to disappear with funds.

4. Lack of Transparency

Projects operating behind closed doors are more likely to mismanage funds.

5. Vague or Weak Tokenomics

Poorly planned tokenomics can quickly lose value as new tokens flood the market.

6. Aggressive Recruitment and Referral Programs

This approach is often used in Ponzi schemes where returns come from new investments.

7. Unrealistic Roadmaps and Deadlines

Overly ambitious timelines indicate focus on hype over delivery.

8. Suspicious or Poorly Audited Code

Unaudited code can introduce vulnerabilities or hidden backdoors.

9. Lack of Community Engagement

Minimal engagement might indicate no real backing or interest.

10. Great Ideas, but Not Practical for an Individual

Some projects require specialized equipment or skills impractical for individuals. Assess hardware needs, skill level, and alternative participation options before investing.
